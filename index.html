Games for Change 2025 (Click me!)

Shizen: A fight for your home

Theme: Nurture for Nature

Achievement: Completed Game and Wesbite, and submitted

In Shizen: The fight for your Home, the theme of environmental preservation is introduced through the story of a once-thriving forest-led village now threatened by greedy loggers. Dialogue with villagers, quests from the ancient tree Shizen, and more uncovered in the forest reveal the essential bond between nature and people that everyone must nurture. The player gathers evidence, water plants, sabotages the loggers’ equipment in times of desperation, and rallies townsfolk—each action reinforcing the urgency to protect what should remain untouched by greedy hands. Your choices shape the forest’s future, making the theme of nurturing one's nature both personal and powerful.

Why?

I created Shizen because of my deep connection with the natural world. Throughout my life, I've witnessed our environment being taken for granted, with few people seeming to care about how our planet might one day reach a point of no return from human mistreatment.Through this game, I hope to demonstrate how even one person, no matter how young or inexperienced, can change the hearts and minds of their community and inspire better environmental stewardship. Shizen isn't just about confronting loggers; it's about reconnecting with the land, believing in your voice, and remembering that every choice we make matters. I want players to feel they're part of something meaningful—something worth protecting. Because in the end, when we fight for nature, we're fighting for our home.

MedPocket - iAmsterdam

Social Issue: Insufficient knowledge of healthcare and financial literacy

Achievement: Completed and Submitted

MedPocket addresses the lack of healthcare and financial literacy by providing in-depth lessons, budgeting tools, and appointment booking features. We brainstormed using FigJam, refined our ideas through paper mockups and wireframes, and used user research and surveys to guide our final app design. The result is a smooth, user-friendly prototype designed to meet the needs of NYC residents.

Competition Experience

MedPocket was born from our team's personal experiences and observations of the significant healthcare disparities that exist in our communities. We recognized that healthcare access isn't just about physical proximity to medical services—it's deeply intertwined with financial literacy and economic empowerment.
Our mission extends beyond simply providing information—we aim to transform how low-incomed communities engage with healthcare systems. By combining practical financial education with healthcare resources, MedPocket empowers users to take control of both their health and financial wellbeing.

Portfolio Reflection

A look at my growth, obstacles overcome, and future aspirations in game and app development.

My Journey

This portfolio not only showcases my journey throughout learning more about developing apps and games, but also my commitment to fully learn and grow in this class. It is a collection of everything I have done, even the successes and the failures made an impact in my life. It shows I am able to complete and commit to any projects that are thrown my way with full preparedness.

Technical Growth

My technical journey began with basic HTML and CSS, but through consistent practice and challenging projects, I've developed proficiency in the complex Python language as well.

Although I was not able to pass the certification, I believe that I was able to learn a lot and will be able to apply my knowledge further in any other coding projects I decide to take on.

Each project pushed me to learn new technologies and refine my problem-solving approach, especially when we did the AI language lessons and it was really informative.

Creative Development

Beyond technical skills, I've developed a keen understanding of player psychology, narrative design, and user experience principles. Competition feedback taught me that great games balance innovation with accessibility, challenge with fairness, and creativity with how user friendly the app is.

Participating in Games for Change specifically opened my eyes to the potential of games as tools for education and social impact, while the InfyApp challenge made me more passionate about serving my community for any social issues that we might face.

My design philosophy now centers on creating experiences that respect the player's time and intelligence while offering genuine moments of discovery and emotional connection.

Challenges and Lessons Learned

One of my biggest challenges was learning to scope projects appropriately. Early in my journey, I would plan overly ambitious games that were impossible to complete within reasonable timeframes, especially for the G4C challenge. Through iteration and mentorship, I learned to identify core mechanics and build around them gradually but efficiently.

Another significant lesson was the importance of user testing. As a developer, it's easy to become blind to usability issues that are obvious to fresh eyes. Regular playtesting sessions became crucial to my team's development process, revealing assumptions and biases we hadn't initially recognized, made all the more easier due to our mentors Rajni and Robina.

Looking To My Future

As I prepare for the next phase of my education during senior year, I'm excited about the possibilities that lie ahead. The game industry is evolving rapidly, with emerging technologies like AI, unity, and extended reality creating new opportunities for innovation that I am very interested in and cannot wait to dive more into.

Ultimately, I want to contribute to projects that push the boundaries of what games can achieve, not just as entertainment, but as powerful tools for education, empathy, and positive change in the world.
